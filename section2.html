Ravenna's Train and Trails
approx 1892-1895
While the natural beauty of Ravenna Park attracted visitors and some homeowners, it was relatively far from downtown. David Denny's Seattle Street Railway was extended to the area in 1892, and named Ravenna Station in 1895. While the Becks' idea for a town was abandoned following the 1893 economic crash, they continued investing in the park. The construction of trails attracted many more visitors, and some considered the park one of Seattle's prime attractions.
